## Human Contribution and Verification

Team members remained responsible for:

* deciding the actual Sprint 6 scope and priorities;
* confirming customer feedback from real meeting notes and trial release results;
* selecting priorities, implementers, and reviewers;
* reviewing AI-generated requirements and action items;
* verifying issue and pull-request links;
* checking repository changes before merge;
* approving pull requests;
* implementing and testing the product (including evaluator fixes);
* conducting the customer meeting and Sprint Review;
* conducting the Sprint Retrospective and validating all findings;
* preparing the presentation slides and rehearsing timing;
* recording the Week 6 Moodle PDF submission and presentation video.

AI-generated content was not treated as evidence by itself. Repository files, issues, pull requests, automated tests, CI runs, deployment results, customer feedback, and UAT results remain the authoritative project evidence.

## Limitations and Corrections

AI tools did not have complete knowledge of the final product state unless repository information was explicitly checked.

The team corrected or rejected AI suggestions when they:

* described unfinished work as completed (e.g., claimed external deployment access was resolved before it was implemented);
* assumed customer approval existed before it was obtained;
* used incorrect issue statuses or milestone assignments;
* suggested closing issues before review and merge;
* required repository permissions that were not available;
* produced generic text that was not supported by project evidence;
* underestimated the complexity of the network access blocker;
* suggested timelines that did not account for university network constraints.

No customer feedback, approval, UAT result, CI result, or test measurement was intentionally fabricated.

## Responsible Use

The team reviewed AI-generated content before committing it. Sensitive credentials, private links, university emails, customer-identifying information, and private access instructions were excluded from the public repository.
